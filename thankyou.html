HARVARD UNIVERSITY | CS FOR BUSINESS PROFESSIONALS

INTRODUCTION

Welcome to my first webpage written with HTML and CSS for an assignment in Harvard University's Computer Science for Business Professionals. This preliminary webpage serves as a token of appreciation to the CS50 staff and is targeted towards those interested in learning more about the course. Anyone interested in the course can find some of the lectures as well as other curated CS50 content on this page.

GRATITUDE

Before commencing this course I was unfamiliar with many concepts in computer science. However, the top-down nature and pedagogy has made certain principles easily understandable. These topics included cloud computing, networking, privacy, scalability, security, and more. Detailed algorithmic processes were emphasized alongside time and space complexities - wherein trade-offs and optimization scenarios are explained. The course structure was a big help into being able to grasp what used to be challenging, abstract ideas and I can say with full confidence I have learned more useful information in this class than most other.

A big thank you to Dr. David J. Malan and his wonderful teaching assistants for all of their hard work in both the preparation and delivery. The breadth of the topics covered are succint and extremely practical. The passion they exude for the subject is evident in every lesson and the feedback in submissions have been extremely helpful.

After progressing through the course content — computational thinking, programming languages, internet technologies, and web development not least among them — my journey into computer science is only getting started. I'm excited to say the least, as the utility for such skills will undoubtedly be a springboard into additional opportunities for future endeavors and a way of expressing some creativity while having fun. Thanks again.
